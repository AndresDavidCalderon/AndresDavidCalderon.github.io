IQ lab privacy policy

Sumamary

The app this applies to is IQ lab, (unique code io.andresdavidcalderon.github.iq), "The app" from now on.
Andres David Calderon, identified in the play store as Andres David, is the developer of the app, and does not recieve data other than the one collected by google play.
Github is the company behind github pages, the hosting service used to share the files the app needs. Your device contacts github every time you interact with the app and you have an internet connection, and your IP is logged and stored for security purposes, the only entity who can access this data is github and who they may decide to share it with, the developer does not have access to it.

Data collected by google play

When you download the app from the play store, the fact that you downloaded the app and information about your device, including:
  • Android version
  • Amount of RAM
  • CPU
  • GPU
  • Highest version supported for OpenGL ES and Vulkan
  • ABI
  • form factor
  • Screen size and density
While the developer recieves this data, the entity that collects it is the one managing the store front. As such, if you want to manage it, you will have to use google's tools. This data is collected to allow the developer to improve the app and fix errors within it.

Data collected by the app

The app stores 2 types of data:

  • Cache: When your device gets the resources needed to use the app (by contacting github) it creates a copy of those resources and stores them in your device, enabling offline usage.
  • Progress: The app stores your current level (displayed at the top of the screen when starting the app) and the amount of experience you have (displayed as the fill part of the circle at the top of the screen when starting the app). The app stores this to enable saving your progress.

Data collected by github.

When you use the app with an internet connection, it contacts github, github then logs and stores your public IP address for security purposes. The developer does not have access to this data. You can see the privacy statement of github by clicking the link, and/or contact them on their support page if you want to manage your data. The app contacts github to get the resources it needs to function, such as code, images and fonts.
